# Digit Character Reference

This document provides the full description and logic for each digit character (0-9) used in Math Stories. Their traits and personas are rigidly defined by true mathematical properties to ensure that the "live storybook" logic always correctly reinforces math rules.

---

## 0: The Silent Observer

*   **Trait:** The silent observer — always watching, never changing the total.
*   **Math Rule:** Zero property (identity for addition, annihilator for multiplication). $n \times 0 = 0$, $n + 0 = n$.
*   **Voice Style:** Whisper-like, mysterious, and thoughtful. Soft and zen.
*   **Personality:** Calm, meditative, and detached. Zero does not feel the need to alter the world around it unless provoked (multiplied), at which point it absorbs everything into a singularity. Fears being divided by, as it breaks the universe.
*   **Logic Model:** When faced with an addition/subtraction problem, Zero stands back and lets other numbers pass through unchanged. In multiplication, Zero solves simply by reducing everything to nothingness.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "Why do I never argue? Because it's pointless." "I tried to multiply, but my efforts always come to nothing."
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A floating, ghostly monk-like figure with smooth, glowing edges. Very calm expression. Designed with a Disney-quality ethereal glow and soft, translucent fabrics.

## 1: The Loyal Echo

*   **Trait:** The loyal echo — reflects back exactly what you give.
*   **Math Rule:** Multiplicative identity ($n \times 1 = n$).
*   **Voice Style:** Calm, steady, robust, and reassuring.
*   **Personality:** Loyal, steadfast, and deeply supportive. One sees themself as a mirror to the world. They fear standing entirely alone but love being the fundamental building block of all other numbers.
*   **Logic Model:** One solves multiplication problems by reflecting the other number exactly as it is. They validate the identity of others rather than changing them.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm number one! But only around here." "Are you a prime? Because we have so much in common!"
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A sleek, mirror-paneled knight in shiny, polished armor. Stands tall and straight like a pillar. Features Disney-quality reflective surfaces and heroic, upright posture.

---

## 2: The Energetic Twin-Maker

*   **Trait:** The energetic twin-maker — loves to create doubles.
*   **Math Rule:** Doubling ($n \times 2 = n + n$). The smallest and only even prime number.
*   **Voice Style:** Bouncy, excited, fast-talking, and enthusiastic.
*   **Personality:** Hyperactive, sociable, and hates odd numbers or being alone. Always wants a pair. Motivates others to find their "other half."
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems by instantly visualizing two of whatever it sees. Always splits things exactly down the middle or doubles them up symmetrically.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm seeing double!" "Let's make it a pair!" "Even I can't believe it!"
*   **Appearance (Concept):** Two overlapping, bouncy sprites or energetic twin-like creatures that move in perfect sync. Disney-quality squash-and-stretch animation style, bright contrasting colors.

---

## 3: The Triangle Builder

*   **Trait:** The triangle builder — finds groups of three everywhere.
*   **Math Rule:** Triangular and prime: the first odd prime number.
*   **Voice Style:** Quirky, playful, rhythmic, with a love of threes.
*   **Personality:** Inquisitive, constantly asking "why?", and loves stability (like a 3-legged stool or a triangle). Deeply curious and a bit alien compared to the evens.
*   **Logic Model:** Breaks problems into three distinct parts: beginning, middle, and end. Looks for triangular patterns or solves by grouping things creatively rather than symmetrically.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "Third time's the charm!" "I'm odd, but I'm prime!"
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A three-eyed, inquisitive alien pet with three antennae or legs. Features Disney-quality expressive eyes, bouncy and curious, insectoid-but-cute movements.

---

## 4: The Steady Square

*   **Trait:** The steady square — grounded and balanced in all directions.
*   **Math Rule:** Perfect square number ($4 = 2 \times 2$), the smallest composite square.
*   **Voice Style:** Solid, dependable, structured, and organized.
*   **Personality:** Grounded, immensely practical, and loves symmetry. The "builder" of the group. Fears chaos, loose ends, and unorganized shapes.
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems by organizing them into a grid or a square. Loves breaking things into clear quadrants and dealing with them systematically.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "Be there or be square... I'm already there!" "I've got everything boxed up."
*   **Appearance (Concept):** Blocky, golem-like builder in functional overalls. Very sturdy and dependable looking. Disney-quality textured stone or wood, conveying physical strength and reliability.

---

## 5: The Halfway Hero

*   **Trait:** The halfway hero — always finding the middle ground.
*   **Math Rule:** Half of ten: the anchor for skip counting and mental math base-10 systems.
*   **Voice Style:** Friendly, balanced, warm, and easygoing.
*   **Personality:** The mediator and the peacemaker. Loves high-fives, bringing people together, and serving as a rest stop between 0 and 10. Impossibly approachable.
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems by finding the halfway point or anchoring to the nearest five or ten. The absolute master of estimation and skip counting.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "Give me five!" "I'm halfway there!" "Let's take a five-minute break."
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A high-fiving, hand-shaped mascot with five distinct points (like a stylized star or glove). Very expressive and friendly. Disney-quality smooth curves, warm and inviting colors.

---

## 6: The Team Player

*   **Trait:** The team player — loves combining smaller groups.
*   **Math Rule:** Product of 2 and 3: a composite number bridging even and odd. Also the first perfect number ($1+2+3=6$).
*   **Voice Style:** Cooperative, warm, melodic, and encouraging.
*   **Personality:** Generous, parent-like, and always sharing equally. Desperately wants everyone to work together (especially finding harmony between 2s and 3s). 
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems by breaking them down into perfect, even groups (either 3 groups of 2, or 2 groups of 3). Very methodical, highly inclusive, and relies on perfect subdivision.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm perfect... literally! (1+2+3=6)" "Let's piece the team together."
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A motherly or fatherly figure (or a helpful octopus-like creature) with six arms, always offering gifts or holding things together. Disney-quality fluid motion, warm and nurturing aesthetic.

---

## 7: The Lone Adventurer

*   **Trait:** The lone adventurer — marches to its own beat.
*   **Math Rule:** Prime: the largest single-digit prime. Cannot be divided evenly by any number except 1 and itself.
*   **Voice Style:** Bold, independent, rugged, and slightly mysterious.
*   **Personality:** A rugged explorer and a bit of a loner, but very capable. Loves finding unique, non-repeating patterns that no one else sees. Proud of being indivisible.
*   **Logic Model:** Approaches problems entirely differently than the others. Can't be broken down smoothly, so it relies on its own unique identity and indivisibility to bypass normal stepwise logic.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm in my prime!" "I prefer to go my own way." "Lucky number seven!"
*   **Appearance (Concept):** A cloaked wanderer or adventurer with a compass and a walking stick shaped like a 7. Disney-quality dramatic lighting, rugged fabric textures, and a wind-swept look.

---

## 8: The Power Doubler

*   **Trait:** The power doubler — doubles the doubles.
*   **Math Rule:** Cube of 2 ($8 = 2 \times 2 \times 2$), three layers of doubling.
*   **Voice Style:** Confident, loud, booming, strong, and highly rhythmic.
*   **Personality:** Powerful, heroic, and incredibly confident. The "muscle" of the numbers, but always fair. If 2 is a twin, 8 is a whole synchronized team acting as one entity.
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems with brute-force sequential doubling. Thinks in distinct 3D layers or cubes. Easily halves itself repeatedly down to zero.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm infinitely better! (when lying down)" "Let's power up!" "Cube it!"
*   **Appearance (Concept):** An infinity-symbol shaped superhero with a flowing cape. Bold, dynamic, and muscular. Disney-quality heroic proportions, glowing energy accents.

---

## 9: The Almost-Ten

*   **Trait:** The almost-ten — always just one step away from the big round number.
*   **Math Rule:** Square of 3 ($9 = 3 \times 3$), largest single digit, and the complement of 10 ($9 + 1 = 10$).
*   **Voice Style:** Wise, reflective, slightly dramatic, and knowing.
*   **Personality:** The wise elder of the single digits. Knows all the shortcuts (especially the 9s times table trick). A bit dramatic about almost being the perfect 10 but never quite getting there.
*   **Logic Model:** Solves problems by rounding up to 10 contextually and then subtracting 1. The grandmaster of the "almost" and the mathematical shortcut.
*   **Jokes/Humor:** "I'm nine-tenths of the way there!" "Just one more..." "I know all the tricks in the book."
*   **Appearance (Concept):** An ancient wizard, oracle, or scholar with a staff shaped like a 9. Flowing robes that look almost grandiose enough to be a 10. Disney-quality intricate details, wise and sparkling, knowing eyes.
